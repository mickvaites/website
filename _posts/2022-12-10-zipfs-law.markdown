Zipf's Law, named after George Zipf, states that the frequency of any word is inversley proportional to its rank.

$$P_{n}\sim \frac{1}{n^{a}}$$

For example; the word 'the' accounts for almost 7%, whereas 'of' occurs in 3.5%.
